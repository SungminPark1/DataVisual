The Data

We found two different datasets, one of these being a collection of education statistics in the United States; at the state level, and the other being detailed information on poverty rates within the united states; also at the state level. The education data was taken from the NCES website, while a study and scholarly article provided by Rachel West and Jackie Odum (State of States Report 2015) provided the poverty data.

The Take Away

When we dove into the data we quickly noticed some rather startling correlations between these two sets of data. First and foremost this underscores the unfornature correlation between poverty rates and spending per students.
